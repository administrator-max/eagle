Glossary
Ann. Yield
The annualised income yield of a position, representing periodic cash distributions as a proportion of current NAV. For monthly-distributing funds it is calculated as the latest monthly dividend × 12 ÷ NAV; for quarterly-distributing funds, the latest quarterly dividend × 4 ÷ NAV. Applicable to Short Term income-generating holdings such as fixed income and direct lending funds. Shown as — for positions that do not make regular cash distributions.
Ann. Return
The annualised total return for Short Term holdings, sourced directly from the BoS performance monitoring sheets. It is defined as cumulative total return — comprising NAV appreciation plus all income distributed since inception — scaled to an annual rate using the formula: cumulative total return × (365 ÷ actual holding days). This metric captures the full economic return of the position to date. Shown as — for Long Term holdings, where IRR is the applicable return measure.
IRR
Internal Rate of Return, the annualised discount rate at which the net present value of all cash flows equals zero. For Long Term holdings it is computed using the XIRR methodology: actual capital call dates are used as outflows, interim distributions (including recallable distributions) as inflows, and current NAV as the terminal value as at 31 January 2026. A negative IRR indicates that, at this point in the fund's life, the current NAV has not yet recovered the cost of capital deployed — common in the early years of closed-end private funds. Shown as — for Short Term holdings.
DPI
Distributions Paid-In — the ratio of cumulative cash distributions received to total paid-in capital. A DPI of 1.00× signifies that the full amount of invested capital has been returned to the entity in cash. Values below 1.00× are expected during the investment and value-creation phase of private funds, as capital is still deployed and unrealised. DPI is a realisation metric and should be read alongside MOIC to distinguish between cash returned and paper gains. Shown as — for Short Term holdings.
MOIC
Multiple on Invested Capital — the total value created relative to capital invested, calculated as (current NAV + cumulative distributions) ÷ principal invested. Unlike IRR, MOIC is time-agnostic and measures the absolute magnitude of value creation regardless of holding period. A MOIC above 1.00× indicates the position has generated positive total value; below 1.00× indicates that the combined NAV and cash returned have not yet recovered the original investment. Shown as — for Short Term holdings.
